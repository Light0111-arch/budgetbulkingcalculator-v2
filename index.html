Bulking on a Budget — Student Guide
💰
Buy in bulk, save in bulk. Oats, rice, lentils, and eggs are the holy trinity of student bulking. Buy 5 kg bags — cost per gram of protein drops dramatically vs. small packs.
🥚
Eggs are the perfect food. At ~₹6 each with 6g protein, they offer an unbeatable protein-to-cost ratio. Eat them boiled, scrambled, or in curries for variety.
⚖️
Aim for 0.15–0.25 kg weight gain per week. Gaining faster usually means gaining more fat. Slow and steady builds quality muscle mass with less wasted effort (and money).
🍛
Meal prep on Sundays. Cook a giant batch of rice, dal, and boiled eggs. Portion into containers. You'll never be tempted to order expensive food when you're tired and hungry.
📱
Track your food for just 2 weeks. You don't need to track forever. Two weeks of logging teaches you what a serving of rice looks like and how many calories your usual meals contain.
💧
Creatine is the only supplement worth buying as a student. It's cheap (₹600–800/month), proven, and will meaningfully boost your strength and muscle gain. Skip the expensive pre-workouts.
🛒
Shop at wholesale or local mandis. Vegetables and grains from local markets are 30–50% cheaper than supermarkets. Buy what's in season for maximum nutrition at minimum cost.
😴
Sleep is free and anabolic. 7–9 hours of sleep releases more growth hormone than any supplement. Students often neglect sleep, which kills muscle growth and increases fat storage.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

✗Bulking too aggressively — you gain mostly fat, not muscle
✗Not eating enough protein — aim for 1.6–2.2g per kg bodyweight
✗Skipping meals and then bingeing — inconsistency wrecks results
✗Spending ₹3000/month on whey protein before optimising whole food first
✗Not progressive overloading — eat more + lift heavier, or it's just fat gain
